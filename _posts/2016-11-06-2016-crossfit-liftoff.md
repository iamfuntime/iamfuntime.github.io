This weekend I competed in the [CrossFit Liftoff][crossfitliftoff]{:target="_blank"}. This is a yearly competition where competitors can compare their lifts against people around the world of similar size and age. This year's event consisted of three events:

* 1RM Snatch
* 1RM Clean and Jerk
* Metcon
  * 15:00 AMRAP
  * 25 pull ups
  * 50 calories on the rower
  * 100 OHS 45#
  * 50 box jumps
  * 25 pull ups

Going into the weekend I was really excited to go in and PR (Personal Record) my lifts and go off on a Metcon. My body had something else in mind. I didn't hit a single mark that I wanted to hit, on the lifts or the Metcon. I was almost embarrassed to post my scores online. That's when I remembered something. Going into the weekend I knew I wasn't going to position well. I'm not that athlete that is going to be competitive in the regional/worldwide level. I recognize that, and when I compete in the [CrossFit Open][crossfitopen]{:target="_blank"} I don't do it because I think I have a chance to make it to Regionals or the Games. I'm not being negative about it, or pessimistic. I'm being realistic. Hell, I saw a kid on [Instagram][instagram]{:target="_blank"}, literally a kid, on Friday Snatch 280# and C+J 340# before going to school. No, I do it because I want to hold myself accountable and see how I fare against myself in previous years and previous events/competitions.

With that in mind this weekend was a success. Last year I didn't even compete in the Liftoff and this year I went and laid it all on the line. I was sore, tight and could barely lift, but I went and did it anyway. Nobody can hold you accountable except yourself, so go out there and put yourself in positions that you may not be comfortable, or where you may not perform your best because the only way to get better is to try.

That said, I'm posting my scores here as well.

* 1RM Snatch: 115#
* 1RM Clean and Jerk: 195#
* Metcon:
  * 0 rounds, 25 pull ups, 50 cals, 100 OHS, 14 box jumps

Not my best showing, but definitely more than I would have done this time last year, or six months ago.
