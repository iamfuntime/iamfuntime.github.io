Tonight I begin my process towards the next level of career advancement. Tonight I get access to the lab and curriculum for the [Pentesting with Kali Linux][pwk]{:target="_blank"} course and the first step towards getting the [Offensive Security Certified Professional][oscp]{:target="_blank"} (OSCP). What does this mean? Essentially it means for the next 60 days my life revolves around work, gym, hacking and my puppy (not specifically in that order).

![]({{ site.baseimg }}/img/oscp-certs.png){:style="float: right;margin-left: 10px;"}
From everybody I know whom has taken this course and test, it is going to be the hardest course and certification I have yet to take, and I've taken a couple pretty difficult exams (Yea SANS, I'm talking to you). I plan on documenting my progress, at least what I am able to document. I know I can't mention specifics about the labs, or the environment; however, I should be able to document any difficulties I'm having, or the throught process I go through...at least to some extent.

I guess what I'm saying is that if I'm distant, or unresponsive, for the next couple months this is why. I'll be neck deep in hacking labs and trying to get this cert.
