I previously [mentioned]({{ "/2017-02-22-i-am-intheopen/" | relative_url }}){:target="_blank"} I was competing in the CrossFit Open this year. As a CrossFit athlete, the [CrossFit Games][cfgames]{:target="_blank"} is the ultimate test of fitness, and the CrossFit Open is the first qualifier for the Games. Now I know I am not a regional level athlete. I got into the game way too late, and wasn't athletic coming in. That doesn't mean that I can't compete for fun against friends at my box, or that I can't compete with myself from previous years, which is what I do when I compete in the Open. I'm not out there to make it to Regionals or the Games, I'm out there to see how much I've improved over previous years.

Going into the Open this year, I was determined to do all the workouts Rx, regardless of the movements involved or whether or not I could actually do them. I would take my low score knowing that I did everything in my power to achieve those movements, or lifts, and focus on things that need attention over the course of the next year so I can hit them next year in the Open. This being my third CrossFit Open, was definitely my hardest. 

### 17.1
_For time:_  
  _10 dumbbell snatches_  
  _15 burpee box jump-overs_  
  _20 dumbbell snatches_  
  _15 burpee box jump-overs_  
  _30 dumbbell snatches_  
  _15 burpee box jump-overs_  
  _40 dumbbell snatches_  
  _15 burpee box jump-overs_  
  _50 dumbbell snatches_  
  _15 burpee box jump-overs_  
    
  _Men use a 50-lb. dumbbell and a 24-in. box_

  _Time cap: 20 minutes_

![]({{ "/img/17.1.png" | relative_url }}){:style="float: left; margin-right: 10px; height: 300px;"}
This WOD was pure engine. I needed to do this one twice, since I didn't like my score the first time. I did it once the Friday night after it was announced, and got time capped at 175 reps. That is 15 dumbbell snatches into the round of 50. The second time I performed this WOD I paid more attention to pacing early on, in order to not get as gassed out in the end. I ended up getting 212 reps the second time, which is 2 burpee box jump-overs into the final round. I think if I'd had one more minute, I probably could've pushed myself into finishing. That said as soon as the clock hit 20:00, I collapsed on the ground. I was shaking for a good 90 minutes after this one.

**Key Takeaways:** I need to work on my endurance. Anybody can race for 3 minutes and do some movements, but these long, enduring, grinder style WODs are particularly not my strong suit.

### 17.2
_Complete as many rounds and reps as possible in 12 minutes of:_  
_2 rounds of:_  
_50-ft. weighted walking lunge_  
_16-toes-to-bars_  
_8 power cleans_  
_Then, 2 rounds of:_  
_50-ft. weighted walking lunge_  
_16 bar muscle ups_  
_8 power cleans_  
_Etc., alternating between toes-to-bars and muscle-ups every 2 rounds._

_Men use 50-lb dumbbells_

![]({{ "/img/17.2.png" | relative_url }}){:style="float: right; margin-left: 10px; height: 250px;"}
Going into this one I knew exactly what my limiting movement was going to be. I still haven't been able to get a successful muscle-up, whether it be bar or rings. My goal in this was to get to the 16 bar muscle-ups with as much time as possible and get as many attempts in as I could before time ran out. What caught me by surprise in this one was just how taxing the 50-ft weighted walking lunges were. That extra 100-lbs was tough to lunge with. I got to the muscle-ups with enough time for two attempts. Not as many as I'd hoped, but enough that I was able to give it a shot.

**Key Takeaways:** I need to get muscle-ups in the next year. I knew going in these were going to be my biggest limiting factor this Open, and it didn't disappoint. It's frustrating not being able to get a movement during a competition, and this was no exception.

### 17.3
_Prior to 8:00, complete:_  
_3 rounds of:_  
_6 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_6 squat snatches, 95-lb._  
_Then, 3 rounds of:_  
_7 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_5 squat snatches, 145-lb._  
_* Prior to 12:00, complete 3 rounds of:_  
_8 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_4 squat snatches, 185-lb._  
_* Prior to 16:00, complete 3 rounds of:_  
_9 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_3 squat snatches, 225-lb._  
_* Prior to 12:00, complete 3 rounds of:_  
_10 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_2 squat snatches, 245-lb._  
_* Prior to 24:00, complete 3 rounds of:_  
_11 chest-to-bar pull-ups_  
_1 squat snatch, 265-lb._

_* If all reps are completed, time cap extends by 4 minutes._

![]({{ "/img/17.3.png" | relative_url }}){:style="float: left; margin-right: 10px; height: 250px;"}
Ugh. Chest-to-bar pull-ups and Squat Snatches!? All week long I was talking about how I needed a strength WOD in order to make up for not being able to get the muscle-ups in 17.2. This was not the strength WOD I was hoping for. C2B pull-ups are not my strongest, and the squat snatch is hands down my weakest lift. Going into this workout I was hopeful I could get to the 185 lb squat snatches. What I didn't foresee was just how tired I was going to be after 25 chest-to-bar pull-ups and 18 95-lb snatches. The first time I did this WOD, I had one attempt at the 135-lb snatch. I didn't hit the rep. I was angry. I was frustrated. I was mostly just pissed at myself. On Sunday I went in and tried again. This time I got to the 135-lb snatch with 2 1/2 minutes to go. Plenty of time to get 4-to-5 attempts in and get at least one rep. Nope...still couldn't get under it. I was angry. I was frustrated. I was mostly just pissed at myself.

**Key Takeaways:** I really need to get back to attending barbell class and get better at my squat snatch. I've got some upcoming goals that I need to hit first, but then I am going to start hitting up at least Thursday night barbell. I won't allow myself to be limited by this lift again.

### 17.4
_Complete as many rounds and reps as possible in 13 minutes of:_  
_55 deadlifts, 225-lb._  
_55 wall-ball shots, 20-lb. ball to a 10-ft. target_  
_55-calorie row_  
_55 handstand push-ups_

![]({{ "/img/17.4.png" | relative_url }}){:style="float: right; margin-left: 10px; height: 200px;"}
Yaaasss! I called this one all week. This was the strength WOD I was looking for. I went in on Friday night with a game plan. I was going to do the 55 deadlifts in sets of 5 with minimal break time between sets. I was going to do the 55 wall-ball shots in sets of 11 with minimal break time between sets. On the rower I was just going to keep moving and do the best I can to get to the HSPU with as much time as I can spare. This actually worked out well for me and I got off the rower in 12:39, giving me enough time for one attempt at a HSPU. I posted up, got inverted, dropped down into a kip and exploded straight up to full extension....I just couldn't get back to the wall and fell forward. No rep as time expired. Not gonna lie I was a little disappointed with not getting the HSPU, but getting through the rower gave me a sense of accomplishment.

I seriously considered attempting this WOD again, but in the end decided I was overall happy with my score. I achieved my objective, which was to make it to the HSPU and have at least one attempt. I will be better next year.

**Key Takeaways:**  still need to work on my endurance. That was probably the hardest 55 calories I have ever rowed in my life. Goals for 2017 include drastically increasing my cardio, and muscle, endurance.

### 17.5
_10 rounds for time of:_  
_9 thrusters, 95-lb._  
_35 double-unders_

_Time cap: 40 minutes_

![]({{ "/img/17.5.jpg" | relative_url }}){:style="float: left; margin-right: 10px; height: 150px;"}
This one was tough. We all knew it was going to be Thrusters and dubs; but that's a lot of reps. I had to do this one on Friday morning because I was going out of town for the weekend and wasn't going to get a chance to do it any other day. My plan going in was to do the first 9 rounds in sets of 5-4 on the Thrusters and 15-10-10 on the double unders. I did the Thrusters exactly as I had planned, but the double unders had to be broken up in to smaller sets based on fatigue. On the final set, I was hoping to go unbroken. I was able to complete the Thrusters unbroken, but had to break up the double unders because of misses. I'll have to work on these more over the next year in order to get better; but seeing as I wasn't able to do any last year, I'll take this as a victory.

**Key Takeaways:** I need to work on my double unders. I could have shaved a couple minutes off my time if I were able to string together consistent dubs under fatigue, instead of getting worn out and having a bunch of misses.

There it is, my 2017 CrossFit Open. I placed in the top 6500 in the SouthEast region, and the top 100,000 in the world. Like I said above, I wasn't doing this to make regionals, or the Games, I was doing this to see how I compared to last year. In that regards, this Open was a massive success. I was able to do every single Open WOD RX, which was my only goal going in. I'll take it.

![]({{ "/img/cfopen.png" | relative_url }}){:style="display: block; margin: 0 auto; width: 100%;"}
Over the course of the next year I will be looking at these Key Takeaways and working on improving on the things I need to work on. Endurance, Double Unders, Snatch, and Muscle Ups. By next year I will be even better than I was this year.
