I figure this is appropriate now that Thanksgiving is over.

At the beginning of the year I went on a major health kick, like everybody does at the beginning of the year, and decided I was going to go to the gym more regularly, and I was going to try something called Intermittent Fasting (IF). As the year progressed, I stuck with the gym, getting in a workout 5-6 days a week, and usually 6-8 workouts. This has been easy to maintain, mostly because I love going to the gym. After a long day at work, or a long weekend, the first thing I want to do is go to the gym and get a good sweat. No matter how I feel during a workout, and I usually feel somewhere between death and death, I feel great afterwards. This makes it easy to maintain, and continue with. IF though...I quit doing after about four months.

So, at this point you're probably asking yourself "What the hell is Intermittent Fasting anyway?". The simple answer is, Intermittent Fasting (We'll just call it IF from here on out) is an eating approach where you alternate periods of eating with non-eating. There are generally two accepted methods of IF: The first method involves a 24 hour fast for 1 or 2 days a week...NOPE, Absolute NOT! The second method is broken up into a 16-20 hour window of non eating, with 4-8 hour window of eating. Obviously, I chose to do the latter and actually really enjoyed it. I quit sometime over the summer when life caught up with me and it was just too difficult to have a social life and continue IF at the same time. I'll need to figure this out once summer time comes around again, I guess the advantage this time is I know what challenges I have ahead of me and can plan for them.

Since I stopped doing IF, I've done some things that have been both good and bad in my journey. Without a doubt one of the best things was a Summer Shred Challenge at my gym. Now I didn't exactly get the results I was hoping for (don't get me wrong, they were good, just not what I was hoping :)), I did learn a lot about how to eat properly to fuel my body for the way we exercise, fat loss, and muscle growth. This should really help me in my process to become a better me as we close out 2016. If you're interested in these Challenges, the gym usually runs a couple each year and I can get you in touch with the right people. No, I don't get anything out of this other than to help you become your best self. :)

So what is my approach to IF? Pretty simple...from 8pm to 12pm the next day, I will be in a fasted state, consuming no caloric food/drink. Starting at 12pm, I will begin my eating phase for the next 8 hours. This doesn't mean I get to binge and eat shitty junk food for 8 hours (although let's be honest here, I'll end up eating some shitty foods). Instead I'm planning on eating mostly healthy meals, taking the knowledge I gained from the Summer Shred Challenge to an IF approach.

The only time where I anticipate "cheating" on my IF schedule is when I workout on Saturday and Sunday mornings. Generally I drink a protein shake right after a workout, gotta get them gains yo! Depending on the type of workout will probably determine if I have a protein shake. If it's a hardcore cardio workout, it'll probably be something more along the lines of Branch Chain Amino Acids (BCAA), but if there's a strength portion, or the workout is strength based then I'm going to want to have that protein shake to help fuel my recovery.
