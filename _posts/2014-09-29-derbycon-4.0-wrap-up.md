I've been to a few cons over the years and the family setting the organizers, staff and attendees of DerbyCon promote has it as hands down my favorite conference in the information security industry. You can literally go up to, and start talking to, anybody at the conference and will be treated the same...a member of the family.

Now to the talks. Thanks to the amazing work by Adrian Crenshaw ([@irongeek_adc][irongeek]{:target="_blank"}) and his team, I was able to spend the majority of the conference hanging out with friends, mingling, meeting new friends and networking rather than cooped up watching the talks. I'm actually catching up on some of the talks I wanted to see right now. :) As usual, DerbyCon had an amazing line up of talks that I wanted to see, some I was able to get into and others not so lucky. Below is a synopsis of the talks that I did see:

- Threat Modeling for Realz - Bruce Potter ([@gdead][gdead]{:target="_blank"})  
 If you haven't been to a Bruce Potter talk yet, go to one. I don't care what the talk is about, whether it relates to your job or not, you go to a Bruce Potter talk. It could literally be titled 'Cleaning up your dog's shit' and you go to that talk because it will somehow relate to what you're doing, or you will relate in some way or another. Bruce has a natural knack for public speaking, and is able to make it all fun and entertaining while getting his point across. His talks are also always on good topics, so you shouldn't have to worry about how to properly clean up dog shit.

- Securing Your Assets from Espionage – Stacey Banks ([@StaceyBanks][staceybanks]{:target="_blank"})  
 Stacey was a first time speaker at DerbyCon and did an awesome job. You could tell she was a little nervous, but thanks to getting Ice'd a couple times during her talk, she was able to calm her nerves a little bit and gave an awesome talk on how to secure assets and how to identify and prevent insider threats. If you get a chance, check out her talk.

- Attack Paths: Breaking Into Infosec From IT Or Other Totally Different Fields – Eve Adams ([@HackerHuntress][hackerhuntress]{:target="_blank"}) and Johnny Xmas ([@J0hnnyXm4s][johnnyxmas]{:target="_blank"})  
 Let's face it, we all have friends who ask how to break into the Infosec field, at least I do...and I never have an answer that I particularly like to give them. Get certs, get a low level job, etc. None of those are particularly sufficient in my opinion. You can go to a week long boot camp, come out of it with a CEH certification but still not know shit and be completely out of your depth when you get hired for a job specific to the skillset that you are certified to have. Johnny Xmas and Eve provide some insight into how non Infosec people can get into the field, learn new things and get that break in that they need. Even as somebody who has been in the field for awhile, I love going to these talks to get some new nuggets on training resources, lab setups, and what other people are doing for self taught training/learning. It's always great material.

- Around the world in 80 Cons – Jayson E. Street ([@jaysonstreet][jaysonstreet]{:target="_blank"})  
 Awkward Hug aficionado, world traveler, hacker. Pick your description when describing Jayson. This talk was originally supposed to be about the diversity between the different world regions within the Infosec and hacking scene. As it turns out, there isn't that much diversity when it comes to the court of public opinion. Hackers are criminals. Jayson flipped his talk to provide some insight into different world regions, some of the different cons he attended throughout his world travels, but he also emphasized that it is up to the Infosec community to change the narrative. To convince people that hacking, in and of itself, isn't a bad thing or a criminal act. Hacking is thinking creatively and getting things done more efficiently. Jayson got a little ranty for a bit throughout his talk, but I loved every bit of it because it needed to be said, and continues to need to be said in order to convince the community to change the narrative and spread the word on what non-criminal hacking looks like. Hands down, one of the better talks during the conference. Plus...there was a very sweet hug between Jayson and Dave Kennedy ([@HackingDave][hackingdave]{:target="_blank"}) during the talk.

I actually only attended those four talks. I had others in mind that check out, but didn't get a chance to for various reasons, but again thanks to Adrian I am catching up on the talks I really wanted to see on YouTube.

Finally, I want to give a huge thanks to the DerbyCon Staff, volunteers, sponsors, attendees and everybody involved. What an amazing conference. It continues to be my favorite conference of the year and I will see everybody at DerbyCon 5.0! Who knows....maybe Waldo will make another surprise appearance.
