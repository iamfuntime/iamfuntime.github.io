In 24 hours, for the following five weeks, I will be participating in the [CrossFit Open][cfopen]{:target="_blank"}. What is the CrossFit Open you may be asking yourself. Put simply it is a five week, five workout, competition held in February and March in CrossFit affiliate gyms and home gyms around the world. Workouts are released on Thursday at 8:00pm EST and athletes have until Monday evening at 8:00pm EST to submit their scores to the CrossFit Games website. 

The CrossFit Open is usually considered a way to test your athleticism, and overall fitness, against other people in your city, state, region, and worldwide, as well as a chance to test your fitness level against where you were a year ago. I am not #InTheOpen to compete for a spot in the Regionals qualifier or even in the CrossFit Games. My one and only goal for this year's Open is to do all workouts as prescribed, otherwise known as Rx, without needing to scale any single workout. I have taken the steps to learn all the movements, with the exception of one, that I was not able to do last year. I have worked on double unders, hand stand push ups (HSPU), chest to bar pullups and toes to bar. I have also worked on getting stronger, and faster, with all of my Olympic lifts. The only movement I have not been successful in getting yet is the muscle up, neither bar nor ring. I know this movement will be in the Open. I accept that my score may be zero for that workout, depending on where in the workout the muscle ups are. I will not be discouraged by this fact. I embrace my shortcomings and will strive to be better next year, no matter the outcome of this year's Open.

There you have it. Starting Thursday evening I am officially #InTheOpen. If you want to follow my progress, you can follow my [athlete page][athlete]{:target="_blank"} or follow the [Frequency Fitness Affiliate][frequency]{:target="_blank"} page to follow my fellow Freqs.
